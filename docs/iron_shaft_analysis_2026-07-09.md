**Date:** 2026-07-09

## Root-cause analysis

### 1. Delivery pattern is the primary driver of low spin
- Static loft (7i) = 33°
- Measured launch 17.3° with attack angle -4.4° → dynamic loft ~13°
- Face is delivered ~20° weaker than static (forward shaft lean, hands ahead)
- Delofted contact produces **both** low launch **and** low spin simultaneously
- **A shaft change cannot fix delivery.**

### 2. Strike quality is bleeding spin and creating variance
- 1.31 smash factor = off-center contact (heel/toe/thin/fat)
- 21 yd carry standard deviation = inconsistent strike location
- Off-center hits reduce spin regardless of shaft profile

### 3. 9i/PW overlap (108 / 107 yd) is geometry + control, not shaft
- 9i loft 41° → PW loft 46° = 5° gap → *should* produce ~10–12 yd separation
- Measured 1 yd gap suggests 9i under-performing OR PW over-performing
- Higher-spin shaft might add 2–3 yd separation via better peak/descent
- Won't add loft

## What Axiom 75 or MMT AMC Red would realistically deliver

Both are directionally correct — higher launch / higher spin than the Taper 75.

**Best-case gains with a proper fit:**
- +500–1,000 rpm spin
- +1–2° launch angle
- 2–3 yd better 9i/PW separation from higher peak
- Slightly softer feel through impact

**Will NOT fix:**
- 21 yd carry variance (strike issue)
- Attack angle -4.4° (delivery issue)
- Pull bias -9.2 yd (path/face issue)
- Smash factor 1.31 (contact quality)

## Cost/benefit

- Reshaft 6 irons at $100–200/club = **$600–1,200**
- Estimated impact on scoring: **modest** — ~10–15% of the total problem
- Risk without fitting data: buying a shaft that swings the profile too far the other way, or provides no measurable gain

## Rank-ordered action plan (highest ROI first)

1. **Lesson focused on delivery** ($100–200)
   - Target: reduce attack angle from -4.4° toward -2° to -3°
   - Target: reduce forward shaft lean / dynamic loft loss
   - Single biggest lever available

2. **Ball audit** ($50)
   - If currently playing a distance/low-spin ball (Velocity, TP5x, etc.), switch to a Tour ball (Pro V1, Chrome Soft, AVX)
   - Iron spin difference: 300–800 rpm from ball alone

3. **Launch-monitor fitting** at Club Champion or True Spec ($100–200, often credited toward build)
   - Bring current 7i for baseline
   - Test candidates: Fujikura Axiom 75, Mitsubishi MMT AMC Red, + 2–3 fitter recommendations
   - Look for: measurable spin gain, tighter dispersion, better peak height

4. **Single-club pilot** (~$100–200)
   - If fitting shows 800+ rpm gain and better dispersion, reshaft **7i only** first
   - Play it one month, validate on-course before committing to set

5. **Full set reshaft** ($600–1,200)
   - Only after single-club pilot confirms real, on-course gain
